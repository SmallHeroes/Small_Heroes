## 1 · The game that started without her
- **premise:** at gan a circle-game ("רכבת" / musical chairs) started while {{childName}} was still pulling on shoes; by the time {{childName}} got there the spot was taken and the game rolled on without them. At night the "I came and it was already going" keeps the head running.
- **central object:** one small **circle-chair** (the empty spot).
- **recurring gag:** עֲנָת keeps almost-sitting on things and lands slow on the floor, finishing her sentence from down there.
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} tries to *replay the day faster* to "catch up" to the game — and the head speeds up, sleep moves away.
- **child agency:** {{childName}} sets a chair "for tomorrow" by the bed — decides to *enter slow* next time, not race the start.
- **visual promise:** a single small chair in lamp-light; slow round panda.
- **why NOT generic:** only עֲנָת's late-arrival gift reframes "the spot was taken" into "you can still enter, slow."
- **risk:** the chair could drift abstract — keep it a real remembered chair.
- **score:** freshness 7 · truth 8 · fit 9 → **8.0**

## 2 · The word that stayed in the throat
- **premise:** at the park {{childName}} wanted to say "אפשר לשחק איתכם?" but the word didn't come out; someone else joined instead. At night the un-said word is "stuck."
- **central object:** a tiny folded paper note in the pajama pocket where {{childName}} wrote the word but didn't give it.
- **recurring gag:** עֲנָת begins a sentence, thinks, tips slowly sideways, finishes from the floor.
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} tries to *swallow* the word ("never mind") — and it gets heavier.
- **child agency:** {{childName}} says the small word out loud to עֲנָת, sets the note on the nightstand "ready for tomorrow."
- **visual promise:** folded note glowing in lamp-light.
- **why NOT generic:** social entry through slowness, not "be brave."
- **risk:** ⚠ overlaps bunny's "say a small true thing" courage lane — keep it about *pace/entry*, not bravery; and the note could feel device-y.
- **score:** freshness 7 · truth 9 · fit 7 → **7.6**

## 3 · The sticker from the party
- **premise:** a loud fast birthday party; everyone got a sticker; {{childName}} hung at the edge and got one last, quietly — but had one small real moment with another quiet kid. At night the party still buzzes.
- **central object:** the **party sticker** on the pajama sleeve.
- **recurring gag:** עֲנָת blinks slow, leans back.
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} replays only the loud overwhelming parts, missing the one good quiet moment.
- **child agency:** {{childName}} peels the sticker and places it on עֲנָת / the bed-rail — "saving the good small moment."
- **visual promise:** glittery sticker in warm light.
- **why NOT generic:** עֲנָת's late-precision finds the *good* quiet connection inside the noise.
- **risk:** stickers/parties are a bit generic; needs the specific quiet-kid moment to live.
- **score:** freshness 6 · truth 7 · fit 7 → **6.7**

## 4 · The corner of the group drawing
- **premise:** gan made one big group drawing; everyone crowded in fast, {{childName}} couldn't find a spot and added one small thing in the corner at the very end. At night: "did my part even count?"
- **central object:** the single **crayon** (the one color {{childName}} used).
- **recurring gag:** עֲנָת's slow lean-and-land.
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} decides the corner bit was "too small to matter" and tries to forget it — which makes it louder.
- **child agency:** {{childName}} sets the crayon by the bed and chooses the one small thing to add tomorrow.
- **visual promise:** a single crayon; a remembered big drawing with one small corner mark.
- **why NOT generic:** belonging through a small *precise* mark — exactly עֲנָת's "late but notices the one thing that matters."
- **risk:** keep the corner contribution concrete and visible, not a metaphor.
- **score:** freshness 7 · truth 8 · fit 9 → **8.0**

## 5 · Last to the gate  ⭐ (uses her canonical pause-stone)
- **premise:** kids raced to be first at the gan gate; {{childName}} was last and felt outside the group. But walking slow at the back, {{childName}} picked up a small stone and saw something the racers missed (a snail / the first star). At night the "I was last" runs.
- **central object:** a small **pebble** {{childName}} picked up at the back — echoes עֲנָת's pause-stone exactly.
- **recurring gag:** עֲנָת literally arrives last to the bed and lands soft, "I'm here. My left paw is already convinced."
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} tries to be faster *in memory* to not be last — speeds up, can't settle.
- **child agency:** {{childName}} places the pebble on the windowsill as "tomorrow's pause" — chooses slow-on-purpose.
- **visual promise:** a pebble on the sill in moonlight; slow panda settling.
- **why NOT generic:** this IS עֲנָת's core — "last ≠ outside; slow is how you see the thing that matters." Her canonical object falls straight out of the premise.
- **risk:** almost none; just don't moralize the ending.
- **score:** freshness 7 · truth 8 · fit 10 → **8.3**

## 6 · Frozen at the new playground
- **premise:** a new group of loud fast kids; {{childName}} froze at the edge and went home without joining, replaying "I just stood there."
- **central object:** a **leaf** {{childName}} held while standing at the edge (pause-leaf).
- **recurring gag:** עֲנָת's "אני… כמעט… מוכנה."
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} reads "I stood quiet" as "I failed."
- **child agency:** {{childName}} decides the ONE small step for tomorrow (say one name) and sets the leaf as its marker.
- **visual promise:** a single leaf in lamp-light.
- **why NOT generic:** ⚠ closest to generic "shy kid" + overlaps koko/bunny shyness — needs עֲנָת's "standing quiet is a real entrance, slow" to separate it.
- **risk:** high drift to generic shyness.
- **score:** freshness 5 · truth 8 · fit 7 → **6.7**

## 7 · The friend who sat with someone else
- **premise:** {{childName}}'s usual friend sat with another kid today; {{childName}} felt replaced and said nothing. At night the hurt runs.
- **central object:** a small shared **toy / bracelet** from that friend.
- **recurring gag:** slow lean.
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} decides "they don't want me" and tries to stop caring — heavier.
- **child agency:** {{childName}} plans the one small thing to say tomorrow, sets the toy ready.
- **visual promise:** the toy on the rail.
- **why NOT generic:** ⚠ emotionally true but ANY calm animal could carry it; risks reassurance/therapy ("it's fine, they like you").
- **risk:** high — fit is weakest; can become "don't worry" sleep-talk.
- **score:** freshness 6 · truth 9 · fit 6 → **7.0**

## 8 · The song everyone knew
- **premise:** at a group sing/end-party everyone knew the words and the fast dance; {{childName}} didn't and stood quiet, feeling out. At night the fast song won't stop in the head.
- **central object:** a small **bell** from the song (or one stuck line).
- **recurring gag:** עֲנָת hums off-tempo, lands slow.
- **emotional mistake:** {{childName}} tries to speed-memorize the whole song to belong — head races.
- **child agency:** {{childName}} hums ONE line *slow* to עֲנָת and keeps the bell by the bed — a slow version is still part of the song.
- **visual promise:** a small bell in warm light.
- **why NOT generic:** belonging by joining *slow*, not matching tempo.
- **risk:** ⚠ can slide into baby_elephant's noise-lane — keep it social (knowing-the-words/belonging), not sensory noise.
- **score:** freshness 6 · truth 7 · fit 7 → **6.5**
