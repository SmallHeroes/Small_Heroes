# Small Heroes — v0.5 Strategic Roadmap

**Author:** CTO. **Date:** 2026-05-24. **Status:** master plan — v0.5 and beyond.
**Reads with:** `docs/PHASE_C_SPEC.md`, `lib/story-generator/STORYBOOK_VOICE_STANDARD.md`, `docs/CURSOR_BRIEF_voice-reviewer.md`.

## The governing principle

We are **not** building "a system that covers everything." We are building a
quality engine that widens along **proven axes**, in economic and product
order.

The trap to avoid: thinking each of the ~12 wizard categories needs its own
from-scratch characterization. `Category × Subcategory × Companion × Direction
× Age × FreeText` is a combinatorial explosion — it would kill the product.

Instead, reusable layers:

```
Story = CompanionDNA + ResilienceTemplate + ScenarioPack + Direction
        + AgeTier + Personalization
```

**The honest caveat:** today every `+` is still a `×`. The recipes are fused —
one recipe = one (companion × direction × template × scenario × age). The job
of the phases below is to convert, one at a time, as many of those multipliers
as possible into genuinely reusable parameters. Until an axis is proven
reusable, treat it as authoring cost, not a free parameter.

**Categories are UI. Templates are the engine.** The wizard's categories map
onto a small set of resilience templates; you never author per-category.

## Phase 1 — widen scenarios within the proven template

After the thermometer line is stable, do **not** jump to a new companion or to
12 categories. Widen *inside the template that is already proven*.

Why: the medical-procedure template has been through the hardest hardening
journey (B.3/B.4/B.5). It is the cheapest place to add real product value.

Scenarios (same companion, age, template, 3 directions): vaccine, dentist,
blood test, cast / broken arm, eye exam.

**Method:** write scenario #2 as a **full recipe first** — then compare it to
thermometer and extract the true Template / ScenarioPack boundary. Do not
design an abstract "ScenarioPack" on a sample of one.

**Important — the next scenario is a product decision, not only technical.**
Vaccine is the technical next-best (closest to thermometer, but different
enough to expose the Template/Pack seam). Commercially, "fear of the dark" or
"first day of kindergarten" might sell more. After Phase C, choose by weighing:
ease of expansion · sales demand · emotional risk · strength of the
personalization feel. Vaccine does not automatically win all four.

## Phase 2 — widen resilience templates

Only after the Procedure template is proven across more than one scenario.

The ~8 universal templates (each covers many wizard categories):
1. **Procedure / Challenge** — doctor, vaccine, dentist, blood test, haircut,
   a performance, a first attempt.
2. **Separation / New Place** — new kindergarten, first grade, a new class,
   sleeping away, moving house, new friends.
3. **Fear of Environment** — dark, noises, the elevator, sea/pool, a barking
   dog, the bathroom at night.
4. **Big Feeling / Regulation** — anger, jealousy, frustration, losing,
   crying, a tantrum.
5. **Social Repair** — a fight with a friend, being left out, a toy taken,
   apologising, fear of joining a game.
6. **Change / Loss of Control** — a new sibling, moving, a parent travelling,
   a routine change, weaning.
7. **Body / Injury / Recovery** — a broken arm, a cast, a wound, mild illness,
   glasses, physiotherapy.
8. **Achievement / Trying** — learning to ride, to swim, a new food, sleeping
   alone, drawing, performing.

These are **hypotheses, not proven templates.** Each new template runs the
full path: design → recipe → B.3/B.4/B.5 hardening → smoke runs (incl. a boy
run) → Voice QA calibration. Never assume what looks good on paper survives
generation.

## Phase 4 — widen companions

A companion is not "the same story with a different animal." Each needs:
emotional DNA, a visual voice, a behavioural voice, **a unique bodily
mechanism the child can mirror**, and limits (what it never does / says /
symbolises).

Bolly works because of a mechanism: `Bolly closes into a ball → the child
mirrors with a hand → the body opens slowly`. A new companion needs a
mechanism at that level (a turtle into its shell, a hedgehog curling, a
chameleon matching breath). Every mechanism must be validated: can a child
understand it and mirror it; is it not scary; does it not read as a therapy
tool; does it work in all 3 directions.

**Timing:** technically companions come late — a 2nd companion with only the
Procedure template is just "the same story, different animal." BUT
commercially, 3-5 companions may be part of the product's appeal. Rule: no new
companion before the story mechanism is stable; but after the Bolly MVP,
companion #2 is worth a marketing / value experiment — it is not locked to a
far-off Phase 4.

## Phase 5 — Router + free text

Only once there are multiple signed templates / scenarios to route into. A
Router with one scenario is theatre.

The Router does **not** write a story. It classifies and extracts safe
details: `free text → Template + ScenarioPack + safe personalization`. Output
states: `supported` · `near_match` · `unsupported` · `sensitive_blocked`.

Iron rule: free text never builds story structure. It (1) classifies to a
supported template / scenario, (2) feeds small safe details into slots. No
signed scenario → do not generate; offer the supported topics.

## Business principles

1. **Small and good beats broad and mediocre.** The MVP covers *enough*
   high-frequency situations excellently — not all 12 categories.
2. **Chips are a contract.** Every wizard chip must be a signed, supported
   scenario. Free text may be `unsupported`; a chip may not — never a chip
   that leads to a fake near-match.
3. **Every expansion must prove value** — parent demand, situation frequency,
   sales potential, emotional risk, authoring cost, reuse potential. Never add
   a companion / age / category / scenario just for "variety".
4. **Sensitive topics are not MVP.** Cancer, loss, trauma, hard divorce, long
   hospitalisation, violence — never free-generated. Future only, and only
   with a dedicated template + a clinical/emotional expert + human review +
   explicit product sign-off.
5. **Run a data loop after the MVP.** Once a narrow MVP is live, measure what
   parents type, which chips they press, where they drop off, what converts.
   The *data* — not a guess — chooses the next scenario.
6. **Tech roadmap ≠ commercial MVP.** Sequence the *build* by reuse; choose
   what to *ship* by demand. Keep the two distinct.
