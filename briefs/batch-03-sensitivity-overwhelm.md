**Category:** SENSITIVITY_OVERWHELM (רגישות יתר / עומס חושי)
**Archetype:** connection (intimate, warm, stays in familiar space. Companion comes TO the child.)

### Story 7a

- climaxType: **emotional_decision**
- Nature metaphor: **שלג שמסרב להיפשר** — a thin layer of snow appeared inside the house overnight, covering everything in the child's room — the floor, the toys, the blanket. It's not cold snow — it's soft, warm, quiet. It absorbs every sound, dulls every color, wraps everything in a gentle white. A music box stopped mid-note. The clock on the wall is silent.
- companionPersonality: slow-moving, speaks barely above a whisper, carries a tiny glass jar that collects snowflakes, treats each flake like a precious thing ("this one remembers a door slamming"), patient to the point of seeming frozen herself, occasionally hugs a snowflake before putting it in the jar
- Stakes: the snow is getting thicker. If it keeps piling, it will bury everything — the room, the toys, the drawings, even the door. She'll be safe, yes. But also alone. And nothing will move or grow again.
- childFirstAttempt: she tries to sweep the snow away with her hands, pushing it toward the door
- childFailure: the snow reforms behind her hands instantly — it's not regular snow, it reforms where it was cleared because the sharpness that caused it hasn't changed. Clearing it physically doesn't work.

- solutionMode: **SACRIFICE**
- requiredFinalAction: she sits by the window for a long moment → places her hand on the latch → companion whispers "it will be loud" → she opens it a crack → one sound enters (a bird) → a patch of snow melts near the window → she flinches but holds → opens wider → more sounds (wind, a distant voice) → more snow melts → her hands shake → the music box starts mid-note where it stopped → the clock ticks → flowers tremble → she leaves the window cracked, not fully open → snow remains in the corners, thin, and she lets it
- forbiddenResolution: snow melting on its own, someone else opening the window, covering ears, running away
- visibleEffect: hand on latch → window cracks open → one sound = one melt → progressive opening → shaking hands → music box resumes → clock ticks → flowers stir → window stays cracked (not wide) → snow thin in corners → room alive again but she chose the pace

- visibleEffect: notices silence = shrink → signals companion → mutual silence → progressive shrinking → stillness battle (almost speaks) → bubbles become droplets → room clears → one final whispered bubble → ceiling pop → walls glisten with tiny drops like morning dew

**Archetype:** adventure (goes outside, multiple locations, physical challenges)

He's now the size of a dog, hiding in a cave, covering his ears. Without him holding the mountain, boulders are rolling down, the paths are cracking, and the whole hillside is slowly sliding.
- companionPersonality: enthusiastic expedition leader with an oversized backpack full of useless things ("rope? check! compass? check! tiny umbrella for a giant? check!"), shouts instructions at maximum volume (making things worse), eventually realizes she IS the problem and starts whispering — which is the funniest thing she's ever done

- visibleEffect: gathering materials → muddy building → companion learning to whisper → shelter closing → silence inside → giant uncurling → inch by inch growth → hand on ceiling → boulder stops → she steps outside muddy and scratched → mountain holds → giant still growing in his quiet room

Right now the sand is a mess — jagged shapes, spinning patches, sinking holes — because the child walked in carrying all her overwhelm from a terrible, too-much day.
- companionPersonality: hands-on sculptor/potter type, always covered in clay dust, sees shapes in everything ("that spike looks like Tuesday's math test"), tries to shape the sand with tools but the sand rejects tools — it only responds to bare hands and real feelings, constantly impressed by what the sand does ("wow, that whirlpool is very you")

- childFirstAttempt: she tries to flatten the sand — stomping on spikes, filling whirlpools with handfuls of sand from the edges
- childFailure: stomping creates angry vibrations → more spikes. Filling whirlpools just feeds them more sand to spin. Fighting the sand's shapes makes them stronger.
- childCost: she must put her bare hands INTO the chaotic sand — spikes, whirlpools and all — and instead of flattening, she must shape. Not fight the chaos, but sculpt it into something intentional. The sand cuts her palms. The whirlpool tugs her fingers. But she keeps shaping.
- solutionMode: **CREATIVE_ACT**
- requiredFinalAction: she kneels → puts bare hands in → a spike cuts her palm → she flinches but keeps her hands in → begins pressing, not flattening — shaping → a spike becomes a tower → a whirlpool becomes a spiral staircase → companion watches, stops talking → child shapes faster → the sinking patch becomes a moat → she doesn't fight the sadness-shape, she makes it a lake → hands sandy and red → the chaos transforms piece by piece into a sand castle that contains every emotion but in structured form → the last shape is a door → she presses it with her thumb → the whole structure settles, solid → the sand around it smooths on its own

- visibleEffect: bare hands in → spike cuts palm → shapes instead of fights → spike→tower → whirlpool→staircase → sink→moat → faster shaping → emerging castle → red sandy hands → final door pressed → structure settles → surrounding sand smooths → she sits back, hands stinging, looking at what her overwhelm built

2. **COST:** Is there a cost felt in body/heart? (not symbolic)

4. **DIVERSITY:** Is the mechanism different from the other story in the pair?
